Signs You Need Driveway Replacement

While repairs work for minor issues, these problems indicate it's time for a complete replacement.

Large or Multiple Cracks

Cracks wider than 1/2 inch or extensive cracking throughout the surface indicate structural failure that patching won't fix.

Age Over 25 Years

Even well-maintained driveways reach the end of their lifespan. Aging concrete becomes increasingly prone to damage.

Sunken or Uneven Areas

Settlement, heaving, or significant unevenness creates trip hazards and drainage problems that require full replacement.

Drainage Issues

Water pooling on or flowing toward your home indicates improper slope that can only be corrected through replacement.

Crumbling or Spalling

Surface deterioration where concrete is flaking, pitting, or breaking apart signals internal damage beyond repair.

Failed Previous Repairs

When patches and repairs keep failing, the underlying concrete is compromised and full replacement is the only solution.

Our Replacement Process

We follow a comprehensive 5-step process to ensure your replacement driveway exceeds expectations.

1

Assessment

We evaluate your existing driveway and discuss options, sizing, and finishes for your new surface.

2

Demolition

Our team removes the old concrete using specialized equipment, minimizing impact to surrounding areas.

3

Preparation

We excavate, grade for proper drainage, and install a compacted gravel base with reinforcement.

4

Pour & Finish

Fresh concrete is poured, leveled, and finished to your specifications with control joints cut.

5

Cure & Seal

Curing compound is applied. After 7 days, optional sealant provides extra protection and longevity.
