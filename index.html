That Crack Isn't Going Away on Its Own

You've been watching it since last season. Maybe it started as a hairline, maybe it was always a little rough — but now it's wide enough to catch a heel, high enough to scrape a bumper, or bad enough that you think twice every time someone walks across it.

Cracked, heaving, and crumbling concrete is the most common exterior problem Tampa homeowners call us about — and it's almost always worse underneath the surface than it looks from above. A crack you can see means movement has already happened. A heaved section means the base has failed or tree roots are winning. And a driveway that looks like it's "just old" may actually be a liability waiting for the wrong day.

The issue isn't just cosmetic. Concrete that's actively deteriorating is a trip hazard. It's a first impression that signals neglect to every guest, buyer, or appraiser who pulls up. And in Tampa, where intense UV, heavy summer rains, and Florida's wet-dry cycle put real stress on flatwork — it doesn't get better without intervention.

What Waiting Actually Costs You

The instinct is to wait — it's a big project, it feels disruptive, you're not sure what it'll cost. That's understandable. But here's what waiting does:

  • Water infiltration. Every crack that opens up is a channel for rainwater to get under the slab. Tampa's intense summer downpours undermine the base, and the wet-dry cycle breaks open the crack further every season. A small crack becomes a major heave within one or two years.
  • Base failure. Once the base is compromised, you're not just replacing the concrete — you're also paying to excavate, re-compact, and regrade the base material. That adds $1–$2/sq ft to every repair you delay.
  • Trip hazard liability. If a guest, delivery driver, or neighbor trips on your driveway or walkway, you own that liability. A heaved section is a documented hazard.
  • Resale consequences. A driveway in poor condition is one of the first things buyers and inspectors flag in Tampa. Exterior concrete in rough shape delays sales and invites lowball offers. You spend more fixing it under pressure than you would have on your own schedule.
  • Limited contractor availability. Concrete contractors in Tampa get booked out weeks in advance during peak seasons. If you wait until the last minute — or until a sale is imminent — you may be waiting longer than you can afford.

The concrete isn't waiting for you to be ready. It's getting worse.

What Getting Your Driveway Done Actually Looks Like

Most homeowners are nervous about a concrete project because they don't know what to expect. Here's the full process, start to finish.

1

Free on-site estimate.

We come to your Tampa property, assess the existing concrete, and give you a straight answer: full replacement, partial replacement, mudjacking/leveling, or repair. We'll tell you what the base looks like, what's driving the damage, and whether there are underlying issues (drainage, tree roots, sandy soil settlement) that need to be addressed or the problem will recur. Written quote before we leave.

2

Demo and haul.

Old concrete gets removed and hauled off. For full driveway replacement on a standard two-car drive, demo typically runs one day.

3

Base prep — the part that matters most.

This is where cheap contractors cut corners and why you see driveways crack within a year of replacement. In Tampa, sandy soil requires extra attention — we grade and compact the base properly, add crushed gravel sub-base where needed, and ensure drainage slopes are correct before a single yard of concrete gets poured.

4

Reinforcement and forming.

We form the slab to your specifications, install rebar for structural reinforcement, and place control joints at the right intervals. Control joints direct any cracking to the scored lines — away from the visible field of the slab.

5

Pour, finish, and cure.

Concrete is poured, finished to your chosen texture (broom, smooth, exposed aggregate, or stamped), and protected during the initial cure. Full cure time is 28 days, but the slab is walkable in 24–48 hours and ready for vehicles in 7 days.

Concrete removal (if replacing existing)

$1–$2 per sq ft, including haul-off. A standard two-car driveway (~600 sq ft) runs $600–$1,200 to demo.

New driveway installation

$6–$12 per sq ft for standard concrete (labor, materials, and base prep). A standard two-car driveway (~600 sq ft): $3,600–$7,200.

Poor base conditions requiring additional excavation and gravel, drainage corrections, thicker slab specs (5" vs. 4"), or difficult access. Tampa's sandy soil often requires deeper base prep than in other regions.

$12–$20 per sq ft. Patterns, textures, and integral color are applied during the finishing process. More labor-intensive, more visual impact.

$500–$2,500, depending on scope. Only appropriate when the slab itself is structurally intact but has settled — not when the concrete is cracked, spalled, or failing.

Questions Tampa Homeowners Ask Before a Concrete Project

How do I know the new concrete won't crack just like the old one?

Concrete always cracks eventually — the goal is controlling where and when. Properly placed control joints direct cracking to the scored lines, not across the visible slab. Proper base prep prevents the settling and movement that causes uncontrolled cracking. And a 4" minimum slab over a compacted gravel base handles residential vehicle traffic for 20–30 years.

Should I replace the whole driveway or just the bad section?

Patching is fine for isolated damage where the surrounding concrete is in good shape. But if the base has failed, patching one section means the next section fails next year. We'll assess what's actually causing the damage and tell you honestly which approach makes sense.

Do I need a permit for driveway replacement in Tampa?

In most Tampa jurisdictions, driveway replacement on your own property doesn't require a permit. Projects that change drainage or involve right-of-way may require one. We handle permit coordination where it's needed.

Can you match the existing concrete if I'm only replacing part?

New concrete won't match weathered concrete exactly — it cures lighter and takes years to tone-match. We'll tell you upfront what the visual result will look like so there are no surprises. For aesthetic-critical projects, full replacement usually makes more sense.

How long until I can drive on it?

We recommend keeping vehicles off for 7 days minimum. Full structural strength takes 28 days. Foot traffic is fine after 24–48 hours.

What if tree roots are the problem?

We address root barriers and root removal as part of the scope when needed. Replacing concrete over an active root problem without addressing the root means you'll be replacing it again.

What Tampa's Climate Does to Concrete — And How We Account for It

Concrete flatwork in Tampa faces pressures that general contractors often underestimate.

  • Sandy soil — the critical variable. Tampa's sandy soil shifts, compacts unevenly, and drains differently than clay-heavy soils in other regions. Without properly compacted fill and rebar, a new driveway on sandy soil can crack within 3 years of the pour. We assess the base on every job and spec accordingly.
  • Intense UV exposure. Florida's sun degrades surface concrete faster than in northern climates. Proper curing compounds and sealing extend the surface life significantly. We account for UV in both material selection and finishing protocol.
  • Heavy summer rain cycles. Tampa's wet season delivers intense, concentrated rainfall. Water that sits under a slab destroys the base. Every pour we do includes a slope check and drainage assessment to ensure water moves away from the slab, not under it.
  • The wet-dry cycle. Florida's dramatic swing between dry winters and monsoon summers causes more base movement than most homeowners realize. A driveway installed without accounting for this cycle will show stress fractures within years, not decades. Properly compacted base with rebar is what separates a 20-year driveway from one that fails early.

We've poured concrete across Tampa, Brandon, Clearwater, St. Petersburg, and surrounding Hillsborough and Pinellas County communities. If there's a site-specific condition in your neighborhood — high water table, sandy fill, mature tree canopy — we've worked around it before.
